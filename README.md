## 📌 Project Overview
This project provides a comprehensive analysis of a survey conducted with professionals and aspirants in the data field. Using **Power BI**, I transformed raw survey responses into a strategic dashboard to identify patterns in income, education levels, and geographic distribution of data talent.

---

## 🛠️ Data Pipeline & Transformation (ETL)
To ensure the survey results were reliable for analysis, I performed the following steps:

* **Data Normalization:** Standardized marital status and gender entries to ensure consistency across the 1,000+ records.
* **Professional Categorization:** Cleaned the `Occupation` and `Education` fields to group profiles (e.g., Professional, Skilled Manual, Management), enabling a clear view of career progression vs. income.
* **Age Segmentation (Feature Engineering):** Developed a custom classification for age groups (**Adolescent, Middle Age, Old**) to analyze how career maturity correlates with bike ownership and financial stability.

---

## 📈 Key Analytics & Insights
The dashboard answers critical questions about the data professional landscape:

1.  **Income Distribution by Background:** A deep dive into how different education levels (Bachelors, Graduate Degree, etc.) impact average income.
2.  **Commute & Lifestyle:** Analysis of commute distances and home ownership, providing context on the "work-from-home" potential and stability of professionals in the sector.
3.  **Regional Talent Mapping:** Geographic breakdown showing the distribution of professionals across Europe, Pacific, and North America.

---

## 🚀 Technical Stack
* **Power BI & Power Query:** Used for the entire ETL process and data modeling.
* **Data Cleansing:** Focused on removing duplicates and null handling for accurate reporting.
* **UX/UI for Data:** Designed a dashboard focused on readability, using slicers for real-time filtering by region and occupation.
